Directed by the original animated trilogy's own Dean DeBlois, the live-action remake is a rare remake that feels like genuine love rather than corporate calculation — beautiful, heartfelt, and surprisingly faithful.

DeBlois Returns to Berk

The decision to bring back DeBlois — who wrote and directed all three animated films — paid enormous dividends. This is a direct retelling of the original story, elevated by live-action tools while honoring its emotional bones. Mason Thames is an inspired casting choice for Hiccup — awkward, earnest, funny. The visual rendering of Toothless, through a combination of practical and digital animation, is breathtaking in a way that creates entirely different emotional texture from the original.

"A rare remake with genuine soul. DeBlois knows what made people fall in love with this story and delivers it with tenderness and craft."

— Variety

Toothless Reimagined

Toothless translates. The animation team created a dragon simultaneously recognizable from the original and newly wondrous in the tangible physical world. The first flight sequence is one of the year's most purely cinematic experiences. At $498M worldwide, audiences clearly agreed.

The Verdict

An emotionally resonant, visually spectacular family film that earns its existence. DeBlois understood the assignment, and then some.
