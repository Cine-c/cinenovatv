Based on Dav Pilkey's beloved graphic novel series, Dog Man is exactly the anarchic, sweet-natured chaos machine children ages 6–12 have been demanding since the books became a classroom phenomenon.

Pilkey's Vision, Animated

The Dog Man books — featuring a police officer/dog hybrid who fights crime with canine senses and human intelligence — have sold 25 million copies worldwide. The film adaptation wisely embraces the books' deliberately crude illustration style, rendering the world as a living picture-book unlike any other animated property. Pete Davidson brings the right blend of enthusiastic wholesomeness. The humor operates on multiple levels — broad slapstick for the youngest, sharp meta-commentary for adults — and manages the balance better than most children's films dare.

"Dog Man is everything children who love the books could want — their favorite scruffy hero brought chaotically to life."

— Common Sense Media

The Verdict

Not for adults without children. Absolutely essential for adults with children who read the books. The 88% RT score reflects a film that knows exactly what it is and delivers it with unmistakable joy. Woof.
