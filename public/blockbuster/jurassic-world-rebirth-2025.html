Gareth Edwards brings his signature awe-inspiring scale to the Jurassic franchise, delivering the most technically impressive dinosaur film since the original while revitalizing an exhausted series.

A Soft Reboot That Works

Rebirth wisely steps away from Dominion-era continuity baggage and tells a self-contained story: a covert mission to collect genetic material from three mega-dinosaurs stranded on a remote island where illegal experiments ran. It's lean, focused, and scary in ways the franchise hasn't managed since 1993. Scarlett Johansson makes for a compelling action lead — tough, practical. Jonathan Bailey brings nervous energy and unexpected heroism.

"Edwards understands Spielberg's original formula at a molecular level — wonder first, then terror. Rebirth is the best Jurassic film in 30 years."

— Empire

Edwards Delivers the Scale

Nobody does scale like Edwards. His signature framing — tiny human figures against colossal threats — gives the dinosaur encounters a weight the previous trilogy never achieved. A sequence involving three Mosasaurs in open ocean is one of cinema's great monster moments of the decade.

The Verdict

A confident, scary, visually extraordinary franchise relaunch. At $735M worldwide, the franchise lives again — and that's genuinely good news for cinema.
