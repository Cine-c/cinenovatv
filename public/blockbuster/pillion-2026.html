Harry Lighton's remarkable debut feature — 7-minute standing ovation at Cannes — is a nonjudgmental, tender, quietly radical romance about a timid man swept up by an enigmatic biker who discovers an entirely new version of himself.

An Unconventional Love Story

Colin (Harry Melling) is a wallflower — mild, invisible. Then Ray (Alexander Skarsgård) appears: the impossibly handsome leader of a motorbike club who takes Colin as his submissive in a dynamic simultaneously romantic, physical, and deeply transformative. Based on Adam Mars-Jones' novel Box Hill, the film is astonishing in its refusal to judge its characters or explain them away. Melling delivers a performance of extraordinary vulnerability and quiet courage — every scene requires complete exposure, and he meets the challenge with total commitment.
