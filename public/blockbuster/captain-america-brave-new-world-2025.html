Anthony Mackie's solo debut as Captain America carries the weight of Steve Rogers' legacy thoughtfully but is hindered by a convoluted political thriller plot that struggles to cohere under Marvel's franchise demands.

Sam Wilson's Moment

Anthony Mackie has been a fan favorite since The Falcon and the Winter Soldier, and his natural charisma fully justifies the Shield. The problem isn't the lead — it's the screenplay, which attempts to weave geopolitical intrigue, superhero action, Hulk mythology, and a new presidential MCU through-line into a single film while servicing a roster of legacy and new characters. Harrison Ford's debut as President-turned-Red-Hulk Ross is a genuine highlight — Ford delivers more engaged energy than he has in years, and his dynamic with Mackie provides the film's best scenes.

"Mackie is the right Cap — confident, principled, charming. The film around him needs a second draft."

— Empire

The Verdict

A foundation-laying MCU film that succeeds in character work and stumbles in narrative construction. Not the launch Mackie's Cap deserved, but a platform to build from. $348M worldwide confirms the audience's willingness to follow him forward.
